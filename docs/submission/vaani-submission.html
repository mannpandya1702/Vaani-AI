01 Clinical stage + the depth of the problem

The bottleneck in rural India isn't the number of doctors — it's the hours a doctor loses to screening, history-taking, and follow-up. We target the front door: Stage 1 — Pre-Visit Capture. Vaani captures a GP-grade history in the patient's vernacular, detects red flags, and routes a structured SOAP summary to a real physician — before the consult.

We deliberately scope to screening & triage. Vaani never diagnoses, prescribes, or signs anything. "The AI is the listener; the doctor is the decider." Because the doctor's time is the scarce resource, we close the loop end-to-end: a single 3-minute call walks the clinical chain — chief complaint → onset → severity → complaint-specific danger signs → age/sex/pregnancy gate — and on any red flag escalates instantly to a Registered Medical Practitioner (SMC-verified, ABDM-HPR-linked) who reviews and signs.

The soul moment (our patent). After the doctor signs, Vaani phones the patient back in their language — "डॉक्टर साहब ने देख लिया है / the doctor has seen you." This closes telemedicine's biggest failure: the patient never knowing whether a doctor actually saw them.

02 Vernacular voice-data collection

The conversation is the dataset. Every screening call is captured turn-by-turn — natively in Devanagari/Tamil script, with Hindi-English-Tamil code-switching preserved and word-level timestamps — and PII-redacted before storage.

Each call is auto-labeled twice: by the deterministic triage layer (band + red-flag categories) and — the gold label — by the real doctor's signed SOAP note. We accumulate a doctor-adjudicated, consented, India-domiciled vernacular clinical-speech corpus.

Collection at scale

  • ASHA-worker-assisted intake at Primary Health Centres + missed-call / toll-free dial-in on basic phones.
  • Stratified to over-sample rare emergencies and atypical presentations (the cases that kill).
  • DPDP-consented per call (s.6), India-hosted (ap-south-1).
  • The doctor's accept/edit on every shadow opinion becomes the agreement signal for future fine-tuning.

03 Data processing, model & system design

Agent-first, prompt-engineered (RAG deferred — by design). Frontier clinical reasoning needs an agent loop with deterministic safety rails, not document retrieval: the safety-critical work is rule-based red-flag detection and statutory refusals that must never be left to a model. We run Claude Sonnet 4.6 behind board-reviewed prompts. RAG (pgvector + multilingual-e5) is the planned layer for grounding to clinical-pathway catalogues as they grow — a retrieval problem, added when the catalogue justifies it. Fine-tuning is the roadmap once the consented corpus is large enough.

Guardrails (no unsafe / hallucinated output)

  • 3-tier triage, safety-first: deterministic rules (BE-FAST, IMCI peds danger signs, silent-MI, sepsis) can only raise urgency, never lower it.
  • Hardcoded statutory refusals (PCPNDT / MHCA / POCSO / drug-Rx) — the LLM can never improvise around them.
  • ICD-10 grounded to a curated rural catalogue — off-catalogue codes are dropped.
  • Drug names never reach the patient (doctor-cockpit only); a real RMP signs every note.

Stack & scale

  • VAPI (web + Exotel PSTN) · Indic STT/TTS (Deepgram/ElevenLabs live; Sarvam ap-south-1 path) · Claude Sonnet 4.6 via a custom-LLM proxy.
  • Proxy = PII redaction + cross-border audit + statutory refusals + prompt-caching (≈57% LLM-cost cut).
  • Supabase Postgres ap-south-1 + edge functions · multi-tenant RLS · 7-yr retention.
  • Scale path: prompt-caching today → Bedrock ap-south-1 (residency + latency) → self-hosted LiveKit-in-Mumbai to drop the platform fee.
